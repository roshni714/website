Roshni Sahoo

Hello! I'm Roshni. I majored in computer science and math and minored in literature at MIT. I'm broadly interested in how we can use structure (from geometry, from physics) to create more reliable machine learning models.

During undergrad, I worked with Igor Gilitschenski and Daniela Rus on uncertainty quantification in the Distributed Robotics Lab and with John Guttag on data augmentation and adapting to distributional shift in the CAML group . I've also interned at Two Sigma Investments and Cruise Automation. In the fall, I will be starting my PhD in machine learning at Stanford University.

In my free time, I enjoy running, practicing yoga, hiking, and reading!

Research
Unsupervised Domain Adaptation in the Absence of Source Data
Roshni Sahoo*, Divya Shanmugam*, John Guttag,
ICML 2020 Workshop on Uncertainty and Robustness in Deep Learning.
paper

We propose an unsupervised method for adapting a source classifier to a target domain that varies from the source domain along natural axes, such as brightness and contrast. Our method only requires access to unlabeled target instances and the source classifier.
Towards Principled Test-Time Augmentation
Divya Shanmugam, Davis Blalock, Roshni Sahoo, John Guttag,
In Submission.
paper

We present a learning-based method for test-time augmentation, jointly learning across many classes which augmentations are most helpful. Our method can easily be applied to pretrained networks and requires no retraining.
Deep Orientation Uncertainty Learning Based on a Bingham Loss.
Igor Gilitschenski, Roshni Sahoo , Wilko Schwarting, Alexander Amini, Sertac Karaman, Daniela Rus
ICLR, 2020
paper / code / video / poster

We introduce the Bingham loss, a loss function based on the Bingham distribution that enables neural networks to predict uncertainty over unit quaternions and thus predict uncertain orientations.

Representation Learning with Hierarchical VAEs
Roshni Sahoo
report / code

We propose a hierarchical VAE architecture for capturing low and high-level features of an image. We evaluate our representations by generating reconstructions of the input image, visualizing traversals of the latent space, and regressing global features from the latent representation. (Project for 6.804- Computational Cognitive Science)
